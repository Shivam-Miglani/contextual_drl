How to make vegetable moussaka
Line the baking sheets with paper towels
Sprinkle salt on both sides of the eggplant rounds and place the eggplant slices on top of the paper towels
Leave the eggplant for about half an hour
Arrange two oven racks in the oven and then preheat the oven to 425 degree F
Remove the eggplant slices from the towel and pat them dry
Remove the paper towels from the baking sheets
Grease both baking sheets with olive oil
Also brush the eggplant with 1/4 some of the olive oil
Arrange the eggplant rounds into a layer on the baking sheets
Place one baking sheet on each oven rack and bake the eggplant at 425 degree F for about 10 minutes
Then exchange the baking sheets' positions in the oven and bake the eggplant for 15 more minutes
Take out eggplant from the oven
Leave them in the sheets as they cool
Lower the oven temperature to 350 degree F
Heat 1/4 cup olive oil in skillet over medium-high heat
Add the carrots celery and onions
Saute until the onions are soft
Then add the mushrooms and garlic
Keep sauteing until the mixture is dry then add the cinnamon and oregano followed by the parsley and tomatoes
Cook until you have a thick mixture then season to taste with salt and pepper
Grease your glass baking dish then arrange half of your eggplant rounds in a single layer at the bottom of the dish
Then spoon half of your vegetable mixture evenly over the eggplant layer then add a third layer made of 2 tablespoons of cheese
Repeat the process to make a second layer using the remaining eggplant vegetable mixture and more cheese
Melt the butter in a saucepan
Add in the flour and stir for 2 minutes
Slowly add in the milk followed by 1/2 cup cheese and some salt and pepper
Finally whisk in the beaten egg yolks and hot sauce (amount depends on you)
Pour this mixture onto the contents of the baking dish
Finally top everything with a layer of cheese 1/4 cup
Bake the moussaka for about 45 minutes or until it is browned on top
